About TEDx


x = independently organized event

In the spirit of discovering and spreading ideas, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TED Talks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized. (Subject to certain rules and regulations.)

About TED

TED is a nonprofit, nonpartisan organization dedicated to discovering, debating and spreading ideas that spark conversation, deepen understanding and drive meaningful change. Our organization is devoted to curiosity, reason, wonder and the pursuit of knowledge ‚Äî without an agenda. We welcome people from every discipline and culture who seek a deeper understanding of the world and connection with others, and we invite everyone to engage with ideas and activate them in your community.


TED began in 1984 as a conference where Technology, Entertainment and Design converged, but today it spans a multitude of worldwide communities and initiatives exploring everything from science and business to education, arts and global issues. In addition to the TED Talks curated from our annual conferences and published on TED.com, we produce original podcasts, short video series, animated educational lessons (TED-Ed) and TV programs that are translated into more than 100 languages and distributed via partnerships around the world. Each year, thousands of independently run TEDx events bring people together to share ideas and bridge divides in communities on every continent. Through the Audacious Project, TED has helped catalyze more than $3 billion in funding for projects that seek to make the world more beautiful, sustainable and just. In 2020, TED launched Countdown, an initiative to accelerate solutions to the climate crisis and mobilize a movement for a net-zero future, and in 2023 TED launched TED Democracy to spark a new kind of conversation focused on realistic pathways towards a more vibrant and equitable future. Our Event ‚Äî TEDx Reel Making Competition

Calling all storytellers, filmmakers and creators! Produce a short reel (30‚Äì90 seconds) inspired by this year's theme "Breaking Silence: Voices That Need to Be Heard". Capture an idea, a moment, or a call-to-action ‚Äî make it memorable.

Who can enter?

Open to students of JSS International School (all grades). Teams of up to 3 are allowed. Individual entries welcome.

Format & Length

MP4 or MOV preferred. Length: 30 to 90 seconds. Caption and subtitles encouraged for accessibility.

Competition Rules

  • Original work only. No copyrighted music or footage unless you have permission.
  • Maximum length 90 seconds, minimum 30 seconds.
  • One entry per person/team.
  • Entries must relate to the event theme.
  • By entering, you grant TEDxJSS permission to show your reel during the event and on our channels.

Timeline

Submissions open: Dec 1, 2025
Deadline: Jan 2, 2026
Winners announced: Jan 7, 2026

Prizes

1st: AED 1000 + feature at the event
2nd: AED 500
3rd: AED 250 + certificates

Judging Criteria

Creativity (30%), Relevance to theme (30%), Storytelling & Emotional impact (25%), Technical quality (15%)

How to Submit

  1. Upload your video to Google Drive, YouTube (unlisted) or Vimeo.
  2. Fill the submission form below with the direct share link and team details.
  3. If you prefer to submit a file, email the download link to submissions@tedxjss.org with subject "Reel Submission ‚Äî [Team Name]".

Note: This form collects details only ‚Äî after submission the organizers will contact you for file transfer if needed.

Tips for a great reel

  • Hook the viewer in the first 3 seconds.
  • Keep the message simple and focused.
  • Use good lighting and clear audio.
  • Add subtitles for accessibility.

Questions?

Contact the media team at media@tedxjss.org or speak to the organizing committee.
